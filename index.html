Statistical Modelling of Count Data with Excessive Zeros and Overdispersion

COMP S461F Data Science Final Project Report 2025-26

Group 1

April 2026

Abstract

In count data modelling, excessive zeros and overdispersion appear frequently in real-life data, which significantly affected the goodness-of-fit of standard Poisson regression models (P). To address these two issues, regression models with different specifications of P were derived, such as negative binomial regression models (NB), zero-inflated regression models, and Conway-Maxwell Poisson regression models (CMP). NB and CMP introduced additional dispersion parameters relative to overdispersion, while zero-inflated regression models introduced additional component addressing the probability of excessive zeros.

The performance of these models is illustrated in an application on a count data from a study on hemophilia patients with characteristic of overdispersion and excess zeros. The result shows that zero-inflated Poisson regression models (ZIP) and zero-inflated negative binomial regression models (ZINB) are the best model in handling overdispersion and zero-inflation, while the more complex and flexible zero-inflated Conway-Maxwell Poisson regression model (ZICMP) underperformed.

1. Introduction

1.1 Project Background

Many medical studies involve real-life data that has outcome variables in form of zero-inflated and overdispersed counts, such as number of deaths or number of patients in a particular period of time. These data are always positive integers and often have a small mean value and a heavily right-skewed distribution that does not follow normal distribution.

In this research project, we studied a dataset collected from a study on hemophilia death factors, where the count of death is the outcome variable and predictors include HIV diagnosis status, blood clotting level, and age. The harmfulness of hemophilia motivates us into finding the best model for our data that can provide concrete discoveries about the causes of death for hemophilia patients.

1.2 Research Objectives

  1. Study the development of count data modelling from the standard Poisson model.
  2. Perform exploratory data analysis (EDA) on the hemophilia data.
  3. Fit the optimized standard Poisson model and perform overdispersion test.
  4. Fit optimized zero-inflated models such as ZIP, ZINB, and ZICMP.
  5. Perform model selection based on hypothesis test results on the model parameters.
  6. Fit the adjusted optimized models.
  7. Compare all fitted models using log-likelihood, AIC and BIC.
  8. Select the best models for the data.

1.3 Significance of Study

This study revisited CMP and ZICMP, relatively new and less explored count data modelling methods, and compared their performance to classical methods such as ZIP and ZINB in handling overdispersion and zero-inflation. The result provides future researchers with a trustworthy example and a practical modelling reference.

2. Literature Review

2.1 Review of Hemophilia Count Data Modelling

Previous hemophilia studies showed that excess zeros are common in such data. Researchers frequently applied ZIP and related models to account for zero-inflation and identify factors affecting patient outcomes.

2.2 Overview of Count Data Modelling

Count data modelling has a long history, beginning with early Poisson applications and later developments such as negative binomial and zero-inflated models. These methods were designed to address overdispersion and zero-inflation, which are common in real-world count data.

2.3 Statistical Models

  • Multiple Linear Regression (MLR): unsuitable for count data due to normality assumptions.
  • Poisson Regression: a basic count model assuming mean equals variance.
  • Generalized Linear Models (GLM): a flexible framework for count regression.
  • Negative Binomial Regression: extends Poisson to handle overdispersion.
  • Conway-Maxwell Poisson Regression: handles both overdispersion and underdispersion.
  • Zero-inflated Models: explicitly model excessive zeros.

2.4 Model Evaluation and Selection Techniques

Model performance in this report is assessed using Pearson residuals, deviance, maximum log-likelihood, AIC, and BIC.

3. Methodology

3.1 Data Description

The dataset contains death counts for hemophilia patients categorized by HIV status, blood clotting factor level, and age group, with an offset variable py representing exposure time. The original dataset contains 2144 rows.

3.1.1 Variable Transformation

Age was transformed from a categorical variable into a numerical variable using midpoint approximation to simplify the model and reduce the number of dummy variables.

3.2 Model Forms

The report uses generalized log-linear models and zero-inflated models. Standard models include Poisson, Negative Binomial, and Conway-Maxwell Poisson, while zero-inflated versions include ZIP, ZINB, and ZICMP.

3.3 Exploratory Data Analysis

EDA includes univariate analysis, correlation analysis, and data validation, helping to understand the distribution, identify anomalies, and examine relationships between variables.

3.4 Test for Overdispersion / Zero-Inflation

Overdispersion was examined using Pearson statistic, deviance, and Cameron & Trivedi’s overdispersion test. Zero-inflation was assessed by comparing standard and zero-inflated models.

3.5 Model Selection Strategy

Backward selection was used based on the significance of predictors in full zero-inflated models. Adjusted versions of models were fitted after removing statistically insignificant predictors.

4.3 Interpretation of Result

Full ZINB achieved the best log-likelihood, ZINB-1 achieved the best AIC, and ZIP-1 achieved the best BIC. Therefore, ZIP-1, full ZINB, and ZINB-1 can all be considered strong candidate models for practical use.

5. Discussion and Conclusion

5.1 Performance of Conway-Maxwell Poisson Models

ZICMP did not outperform ZIP or ZINB in this dataset. Although it provides greater flexibility, its complexity may reduce practical advantage in this case.

5.2 Death Factors for Hemophilia Patients

The results suggest that HIV-positive patients and older patients are associated with higher death counts. This provides evidence that these groups may require more treatment priority when healthcare resources are limited.

5.3 Future Research Directions

Future work may compare quasi-Poisson, NB1, and zero-inflated geometric models, and further investigate computational issues related to ZICMP.

5.4 Conclusion

This study demonstrated a complete workflow for count data modelling under overdispersion and zero-inflation. The results showed that adjusted ZIP and adjusted ZINB were the best-performing models overall, while ZICMP, despite its flexibility, did not provide superior performance here.
